# Analysis Protocols

Detailed step-by-step protocols for reviewing key contract areas. Apply each protocol during the clause-by-clause review (Step 3 of the workflow).

---

## Contract Header Review Checklist

| Item | Verify | Standard Requirement |
|------|--------|---------------------|
| Parties | Full legal names, not nicknames or abbreviations | Must match IDs; verify entity type (LLC, Corp, individual) |
| Effective Date | Clearly stated; not contingent on undefined events | Must be a date certain or "upon execution by both parties" |
| Term/Duration | Fixed term with clear start and end dates | Should not exceed remaining eligibility without justification |
| Definitions Section | Key terms defined (Deliverables, Marks, Territory, etc.) | Undefined terms create ambiguity; flag if missing |
| Recitals | Accurately describe the deal purpose | Should not contain binding obligations disguised as recitals |
| Athlete Capacity | Identifies athlete as independent contractor | Reject employment classification; verify tax treatment |
| Agent/Intermediary | If present, verify authority and registration | Must be registered per applicable state law |

## Compensation Analysis Protocol

**For each compensation element, conduct the following analysis:**

### Step 1: Identify All Compensation Components
- Base fee / flat rate
- Per-deliverable payments
- Performance bonuses (social media metrics, sales, etc.)
- Royalties or revenue sharing
- In-kind compensation (products, services, experiences)
- Expense reimbursement

### Step 2: Assess Payment Certainty
| Component | Guaranteed? | Conditions | Risk Level |
|-----------|-------------|------------|------------|
| [Each component] | Yes / No / Partial | [What triggers payment] | High / Medium / Low |

### Step 3: Evaluate Payment Terms
- When is payment due? (Net 30, Net 60, upon delivery, etc.)
- What triggers payment? (Deliverable completion, publication, campaign end)
- What happens if brand delays or fails to pay? (Late fees, remedies)
- Are there clawback or offset provisions? (Flag if yes)

### Step 4: Determine Fair Market Value Assessment
- Is compensation commensurate with the scope of rights granted?
- Does the compensation reflect the athlete's current market value?
- Are there hidden costs borne by the athlete? (Travel, wardrobe, production)
- Flag any "exposure" or "experience" offered as compensation substitutes

## Likeness Rights Analysis Protocol

**For each rights grant, conduct the following analysis:**

### Step 1: Scope the Grant
| Element | What Contract Says | Acceptable? |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Rights Granted** | Name / Image / Likeness / Voice / Signature | Narrowly tailored to deal? |
| **Media/Channels** | Social media / Print / Digital / Broadcast / All media | Proportional to compensation? |
| **Territory** | Local / State / National / Worldwide | Proportional to compensation? |
| **Duration of Use** | Contract term only / Extended / Perpetual | Must revert at termination |
| **Exclusivity** | Exclusive / Non-exclusive | If exclusive, narrowly defined? |

### Step 2: Assess Control Retained
- Does the athlete approve content before publication?
- Can the athlete request takedown of specific content?
- Does the athlete retain ownership of their personal social media accounts?
- Can the brand modify content after initial approval?
- Can the brand create AI-generated or synthetic content using the athlete's likeness?

### Step 3: Evaluate Transferability
- Can the brand sublicense rights to third parties?
- Can the brand assign the contract without athlete consent?
- Are sublicensees bound by the same restrictions?
- Does the athlete know who may ultimately use their likeness?

### Step 4: Determine Post-Termination Obligations
- How long can the brand use existing content after termination? (Wind-down period)
- Must the brand remove/delete content after termination?
- Do any rights survive termination? (Flag if yes — should be narrow)
- What about content already "in the wild" (shared, reposted by third parties)?

## Termination Clause Analysis Protocol

### Step 1: Identify All Termination Triggers

| Trigger | Available To | Notice Required | Consequences |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Convenience (no cause) | Athlete / Brand / Both | [Days] | [Penalties?] |
| Material breach | Athlete / Brand / Both | [Cure period] | [Remedies] |
| NCAA eligibility loss | Athlete / Brand / Both | [Notice] | [Effect] |
| Moral character / conduct | Brand only? | [Process] | [Subjective?] |
| Force majeure | Both | [Notice] | [Effect] |
| Bankruptcy / insolvency | Both | [Automatic?] | [Effect] |

### Step 2: Assess Balance
- Can the athlete exit as easily as the brand?
- Are cure periods reasonable and symmetric?
- Are termination penalties proportionate?

### Step 3: Evaluate Post-Termination
- What obligations survive? (Confidentiality, non-compete, IP restrictions)
- How long do survival provisions last?
- Is earned but unpaid compensation still owed?
- Must the brand return athlete-provided materials?
