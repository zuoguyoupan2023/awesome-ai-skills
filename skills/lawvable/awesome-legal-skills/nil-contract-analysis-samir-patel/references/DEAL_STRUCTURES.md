# Deal Structures & Collective Deals

## Common NIL Deal Structure Reference

| Deal Type | Key Risk Areas | Primary Concerns |
|-----------|---------------|------------------|
| **Social Media Endorsement** | Content volume, approval rights, usage beyond athlete's channels | Perpetual content rights, repurposing without consent |
| **Personal Appearance** | Scheduling conflicts, travel obligations, cancellation penalties | Athlete bearing travel costs, unreasonable cancellation fees |
| **Merchandise / Licensing** | Royalty rates, audit rights, quality control | Below-market royalties, no minimum guarantees, no quality approval |
| **Autograph / Memorabilia** | Exclusivity scope, event frequency, inventory rights | Overbroad exclusivity, unlimited signing obligations |
| **Camp / Clinic** | Liability, insurance, institutional approval | Athlete bearing event liability, no insurance requirement |
| **Group Licensing / Collective** | Revenue sharing, governance, opt-out | Opaque distributions, no individual consent, no exit mechanism |
| **Brand Ambassador** | Scope creep, exclusivity breadth, term length | Vague deliverables expanding over time, broad category lockout |

## Group Licensing / NIL Collective Deals

Additional requirements when the contract involves a NIL collective, group licensing arrangement, or multi-athlete deal:

### 1. Revenue Sharing Transparency
- Distribution formula must be clearly stated (equal share, performance-based, tiered)
- Athlete must be able to verify calculations (audit rights or regular accounting statements)
- Administrative fees and collective overhead must be disclosed and capped

### 2. Individual vs. Collective Obligations
- Differentiate what the athlete owes individually vs. what the collective manages
- Athlete should not be liable for other athletes' failures to perform
- Individual marketing obligations must be clearly scoped

### 3. Opt-Out Mechanisms
- Athlete must have the right to withdraw from the collective with reasonable notice
- Withdrawal should not trigger forfeiture of earned compensation
- Assess whether opt-out affects other individual NIL deals

### 4. Distribution Methodology
- Verify the timing and method of distributions (monthly, quarterly, per-event)
- Flag any discretionary distribution language ("in the collective's sole discretion")
- Confirm whether distributions are guaranteed minimums or variable

### 5. Tax Treatment
- Verify whether the athlete is classified as independent contractor
- Flag any language suggesting employment relationship
- Confirm whether the collective issues 1099s and the expected timing

### 6. Multi-Athlete Consent and Governance
- Can the collective bind the athlete to new deals without individual consent?
- Does the athlete have a vote or voice in collective decisions?
- Who controls the collective's brand partnerships and deal flow?

### 7. Compliance Coordination
- Is the collective registered and compliant with state NIL laws?
- Does the collective handle institutional disclosure on the athlete's behalf?
- What happens if the collective loses compliance status mid-term?

## Document Request Checklist

### From the Brand/Company
- Executed or final draft contract with all exhibits and schedules
- Brand guidelines and content standards (if referenced in contract)
- Prior NIL deals with this athlete or similar athletes (for market comparison, if available)
- Proof of entity formation and good standing
- Evidence of insurance (if brand is requiring athlete appearances or events)
- Campaign brief or scope of work (if referenced but not attached)

### From the Athlete/Family
- Current team/institutional contract (to check for conflicts)
- List of existing NIL deals (to check exclusivity conflicts)
- Institutional NIL policy (from compliance office)
- Agent representation agreement (if applicable)
- Athlete's NIL disclosure submissions to date
- Any prior correspondence with the brand

### From the Institution
- Current institutional NIL policy
- Required disclosure forms and timelines
- Any pre-approval requirements
- List of prohibited brand categories (if any)
- Institutional compliance office contact information

### From the Collective (If Group Licensing / Collective Deal)
- Collective's organizational documents (formation, governance)
- Distribution methodology documentation
- State registration and compliance records
- Most recent financial statements or accounting reports
- List of current brand partners and active campaigns
- Template individual participation agreement
