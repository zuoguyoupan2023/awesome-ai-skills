# Florida NIL Compliance Reference

## Quick Reference: Florida NIL Law (FL SB 646 / F.S. 1006.74)

| Matter | Florida Requirement |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Effective Statute** | F.S. § 1006.74 (as amended); originally FL SB 646 |
| **Eligible Athletes** | Intercollegiate student-athletes at Florida postsecondary institutions |
| **Institutional Disclosure** | Athlete must disclose NIL contracts to institution; institution may require advance notice |
| **Prohibited Conflicts** | NIL contract cannot conflict with the athlete's team contract |
| **Agent Registration** | Agents and intermediaries must be registered per FL law (F.S. Chapter 468, Part VIII) |
| **Institutional Involvement** | Institution cannot act as athlete's agent or arrange NIL deals directly |
| **Prohibited Categories** | Contracts cannot promote products/services prohibited by institutional policy |
| **Compensation Structure** | Must be for legitimate NIL use; cannot be disguised pay-for-play or enrollment inducement |
| **Duration** | Should be reasonable relative to athlete's remaining eligibility |
| **Legal Representation** | Athlete has the right to obtain legal representation for contract review |
| **Tax Obligations** | Athlete is responsible for applicable taxes; contract should clarify tax treatment |

## Florida Compliance Review Checklist

When the contract is governed by Florida law or involves a Florida institution/athlete, check each of the following:

### Statutory Compliance (F.S. 1006.74)

| Requirement | What to Check | Non-Compliant If... |
|-------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Institutional disclosure | Contract permits/requires athlete to disclose to institution | Confidentiality clause blocks institutional disclosure |
| No team contract conflict | Terms align with athlete's existing team contract | Exclusivity, scheduling, or branding terms conflict with team obligations |
| Agent registration | Agent/intermediary is registered per F.S. Chapter 468, Part VIII | Agent is unregistered or registration cannot be verified |
| No institutional involvement | Institution is not a party to, facilitator of, or beneficiary of the deal | Institution arranged, directed, or financially benefits from the deal |
| Prohibited categories | Deal does not promote products prohibited by institutional policy | Contract promotes alcohol, tobacco, gambling, cannabis, or other prohibited categories |
| Legitimate NIL use | Compensation is for genuine use of name, image, or likeness | Compensation structure resembles pay-for-play or enrollment inducement |
| Reasonable duration | Term is proportionate to athlete's remaining eligibility | Term substantially exceeds remaining eligibility without justification |
| Right to counsel | Contract does not restrict athlete's right to legal representation | Contract waives or limits athlete's right to independent legal review |

### Institutional Policy Compliance
- Does the contract conflict with the institution's published NIL policy?
- Does the institution require advance approval or notification before execution?
- Are there institutional-specific prohibited brand categories beyond state law?
- Does the contract's scheduling requirements conflict with team activities?
- Is the institution's compliance office aware of and able to review the deal?

### NCAA Bylaw Compliance
- Could any term jeopardize the athlete's NCAA eligibility?
- Does the compensation structure risk being classified as an impermissible benefit?
- Are there booster involvement concerns that could trigger NCAA scrutiny?
- Does the contract require athletic performance or enrollment as a condition of payment?
- Could the deal be interpreted as an inducement to attend or remain at a specific institution?
