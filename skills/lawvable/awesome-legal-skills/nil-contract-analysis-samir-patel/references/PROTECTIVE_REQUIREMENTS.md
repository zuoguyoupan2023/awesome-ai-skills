# Standard Athlete Protective Requirements

The following requirements apply to all NIL contracts unless the reviewing attorney determines otherwise based on the specific deal structure.

---

## 1. Compensation Clarity
- **Requirement:** Compensation must be stated in specific dollar amounts with a defined payment schedule
- **Action:** Reject vague language such as "reasonable compensation," "to be determined," or "based on performance metrics" without defined formulas
- **Note:** Each deliverable (social media post, appearance, likeness use) should map to a specific payment amount and due date

## 2. Likeness Usage Approval Rights
- **Requirement:** Athlete must have prior written approval over all content using their name, image, or likeness before publication
- **Action:** Reject any blanket grant that allows the brand to create and publish content without athlete review
- **Note:** Approval rights should cover initial use AND any subsequent modifications, edits, or derivative content

## 3. Termination for Convenience
- **Requirement:** Athlete must have the right to terminate the agreement with reasonable written notice (30 days or less)
- **Action:** Reject contracts with no athlete termination right or that only permit termination for cause
- **Note:** Termination should not trigger penalties, liquidated damages, or forfeiture of earned compensation

## 4. Term Limits and Renewal Controls
- **Requirement:** Contract duration should not exceed the athlete's remaining NCAA eligibility without clear justification
- **Action:** Reject automatic renewal clauses that lack adequate advance notice (minimum 30 days) and athlete opt-out
- **Note:** If the contract extends beyond eligibility, verify the athlete understands the post-eligibility commitment

## 5. Exclusivity Scope Limits
- **Requirement:** Any exclusivity must be narrowly defined by product category, not industry-wide or blanket
- **Action:** Reject exclusivity provisions that prevent the athlete from entering NIL deals in unrelated categories
- **Note:** Exclusivity should not extend beyond the contract term; post-term non-competes are disfavored

## 6. Intellectual Property Reversion
- **Requirement:** All rights to the athlete's name, image, and likeness must revert to the athlete upon termination or expiration
- **Action:** Reject perpetual or irrevocable IP grants; require a defined reversion date and content takedown obligation
- **Note:** The brand should be required to cease all use and remove published content within a defined period (30 days or less)

## 7. Indemnification Mutuality
- **Requirement:** Indemnification obligations must be mutual — both parties indemnify for their own acts, omissions, and breaches
- **Action:** Reject one-sided indemnification that only protects the brand/company
- **Note:** Athlete's indemnification exposure should be capped (ideally at the total compensation received under the contract)

## 8. Institutional Disclosure Compliance
- **Requirement:** The contract must contain language permitting (or requiring) the athlete to disclose the agreement to their institution as required by NCAA rules and institutional policy
- **Action:** Reject any confidentiality provision that would prevent required institutional disclosures
- **Note:** Many institutions require advance disclosure or approval; the contract should not conflict with these obligations

## 9. Governing Law and Venue
- **Requirement:** Governing law and venue should be in the athlete's home state or the state where the institution is located
- **Action:** Flag any governing law or venue provision requiring the athlete to litigate in a distant or unfavorable jurisdiction
- **Note:** If arbitration is required, verify the forum is neutral and the arbitration costs are not disproportionately borne by the athlete
