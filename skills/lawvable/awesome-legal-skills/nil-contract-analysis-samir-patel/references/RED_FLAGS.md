# Red Flag Categories

Analyze the contract for issues in each of the following categories. For each issue found, assign a severity: **HIGH**, **MEDIUM**, or **LOW**.

---

## Red Flag Analysis Protocol

**For EACH red flag identified, conduct the following analysis:**

### Step 1: Identify the Issue
- Quote or paraphrase the specific contract language
- Note the section/clause number

### Step 2: Assess Severity
- **HIGH**: Creates immediate legal risk, potential eligibility impact, or significant financial exposure
- **MEDIUM**: Creates unfavorable terms that should be negotiated but are not deal-breakers
- **LOW**: Minor issues or missing best practices that should be addressed if possible

### Step 3: Determine Impact on Athlete
- Financial impact (lost compensation, unexpected costs, liability exposure)
- Rights impact (loss of control over likeness, restricted future opportunities)
- Compliance impact (NCAA eligibility risk, institutional policy conflict)
- Practical impact (unreasonable obligations, burdensome requirements)

### Step 4: Recommend Action (Preferred Redline + Fallback)

For each red flag, provide BOTH a preferred redline and a fallback position. This gives the attorney a negotiation ladder — lead with preferred, concede to fallback if needed.

| Clause Pattern | Preferred Redline | Fallback Position |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Perpetual rights grant | Delete entirely; require reversion at termination + content removal within 15 days | Cap at 6 months post-termination wind-down; content removal within 90 days |
| Blanket exclusivity | Narrow to specific product SKU or named product line | Narrow to specific product category; exclude unrelated verticals |
| One-sided indemnification | Mutual indemnification; athlete's liability capped at compensation received | Mutual indemnification; athlete's liability capped at 2x compensation received |
| No payment timeline | Net 15 with 1.5% monthly late fee and right to suspend performance | Net 30 with late payment penalties at statutory rate |
| Vague moral clause | Delete moral clause entirely | Narrow to criminal conviction of felony or material breach only |
| Auto-renewal without notice | Delete auto-renewal; require affirmative opt-in for each renewal | Auto-renewal with 60-day advance written notice and athlete opt-out right |
| Distant venue/governing law | Athlete's home state for both governing law and venue | Athlete's institution state; or defendant's home state |
| No termination for convenience | Add 15-day written notice termination right for athlete | Add 30-day written notice termination right for athlete |
| Confidentiality blocking disclosure | Carve out all institutional, NCAA, and regulatory disclosures; add legal counsel carve-out | Carve out institutional and NCAA disclosures only |
| Assignment without consent | Prohibit assignment entirely without athlete's prior written consent | Require prior written consent; consent not unreasonably withheld |
| No AI/synthetic content restriction | Prohibit all AI-generated or synthetic content using athlete's likeness | Require separate written consent for each AI/synthetic use |
| Sublicensing to unnamed parties | Prohibit sublicensing entirely | Allow sublicensing only to named affiliates with same restrictions |

## Category 1: Compensation & Payment
- Vague or missing compensation amounts
- No payment timeline or milestones
- "Exposure" or "experience" offered in lieu of payment
- Clawback provisions that could eliminate earned compensation
- Offsets or deductions not clearly defined
- No late payment penalties or remedies for the athlete
- In-kind compensation without stated fair market value
- Performance bonuses tied to metrics outside athlete's control

## Category 2: Exclusivity & Non-Compete
- Overbroad exclusivity (category, industry, or blanket)
- Exclusivity period extending beyond the contract term
- Non-compete clauses restricting post-contract earning ability
- Right of first refusal without clear terms or expiration
- Exclusivity that conflicts with existing athlete obligations
- Category definitions that are vague or overly broad ("lifestyle," "sports," "wellness")

## Category 3: Intellectual Property & Likeness Rights
- Perpetual or irrevocable grants of name/image/likeness rights
- Rights that extend beyond the contract term without additional compensation
- Broad "derivative works" language allowing unlimited content creation
- No approval rights over how the athlete's likeness is used
- Transfer or sublicense of likeness rights to unnamed third parties
- No restrictions on AI-generated or synthetic content using the athlete's likeness
- No content removal obligation post-termination
- Voice, signature, or biometric data included without specific consent

## Category 4: Termination & Exit
- No termination for convenience clause for the athlete
- Unreasonable cure periods (>30 days)
- Termination penalties or liquidated damages disproportionate to contract value
- Survival clauses that keep onerous obligations alive post-termination
- Automatic renewal without adequate notice provisions
- No termination right upon NCAA eligibility loss
- Brand-only termination rights (asymmetric)
- No obligation to pay earned compensation upon early termination

## Category 5: Indemnification & Liability
- One-sided indemnification favoring only the brand/company
- Athlete required to indemnify for brand's own negligence
- No cap on indemnification liability
- Broad "hold harmless" language without mutual obligations
- Athlete bearing liability for brand's regulatory violations
- No insurance requirements for the brand
- Indemnification surviving indefinitely post-termination

## Category 6: Representations & Warranties
- Athlete warranting matters outside their control
- Overly broad moral character or conduct clauses
- Social media behavior clauses with vague standards ("conduct unbecoming")
- Warranties that survive indefinitely
- Athlete warranting they will maintain specific social media follower counts
- Brand's right to terminate based on subjective "brand fit" determinations

## Category 7: Governing Law & Dispute Resolution
- Governing law in a jurisdiction unfavorable to the athlete
- Mandatory arbitration with biased forum selection
- Waiver of jury trial rights without clear consideration
- Class action waivers
- Venue selection requiring travel to distant jurisdictions
- No prevailing party attorney's fees provision
- Arbitration costs borne solely by the athlete
- Shortened statute of limitations

## Category 8: State-Specific NIL Compliance
Apply the matching state compliance reference file (e.g., `FLORIDA_COMPLIANCE.md`, `NEW_YORK_COMPLIANCE.md`) for jurisdiction-specific red flags. The following general red flags apply regardless of state:
- Contract conflicts with the athlete's team contract or institutional policy
- Agent or facilitator not registered per applicable state requirements
- Missing disclosure obligations to the athlete's institution
- Terms that could jeopardize the athlete's NCAA eligibility
- Contract duration exceeding the athlete's remaining eligibility without justification
- Compensation structure that could be construed as pay-for-play
- No provision addressing what happens if NIL rules change during the term
- Failure to comply with institutional notification timelines

## Category 9: General Contract Deficiencies
- Missing or incomplete definitions section
- Ambiguous force majeure provisions
- No confidentiality or overly broad confidentiality obligations
- Missing notice provisions or unreasonable notice requirements
- Assignment rights without athlete consent
- Entire agreement clause missing or poorly drafted
- No severability clause
- No amendment provision requiring written mutual consent
- Missing counterparts/electronic signature clause

## Immediate Escalation Triggers

The following matters require immediate attention and potential escalation to the reviewing attorney or the athlete's family:

### Contract Red Flags
- Perpetual or irrevocable grant of likeness rights with no reversion
- No compensation or nominal compensation for substantial rights granted
- Athlete personally guaranteeing obligations or waiving legal rights
- Contract requires athlete to promote products that may violate NCAA rules (alcohol, tobacco, gambling, cannabis)
- Assignment clause allowing transfer to any third party without consent
- Non-compete that would restrict athlete's professional career post-NCAA
- Indemnification with no cap that could expose athlete to unlimited liability
- Confidentiality clause that prevents athlete from seeking legal advice about the contract
- Penalty clauses triggered by loss of eligibility or injury

### Compliance Red Flags
- Contract appears to be a pay-for-play arrangement disguised as NIL
- Terms conflict with known NCAA bylaws or recent guidance
- Agent or intermediary is not registered in the applicable state
- Contract prohibits required institutional disclosures
- Compensation is contingent on athletic performance or enrollment at a specific institution
- Contract involves a booster or entity with institutional ties that may trigger NCAA scrutiny
- No compliance review or institutional approval has been obtained
