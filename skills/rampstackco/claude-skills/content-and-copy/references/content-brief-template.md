# Content Brief Template

Every piece of content needs a brief before writing. Without one, the piece will drift, miss the audience, or fail the goal.

A brief takes 15 minutes to write. Saves hours of rewrites later.

---

## Template

```markdown
# Brief: [Working title]

## Reader

Who specifically is this for? Be precise.

- Primary audience segment: [from content strategy]
- What they currently know: [their starting point]
- What they want to know: [the question they're bringing]
- What they need to do after: [the action this enables]

## Goal

What does this piece accomplish?

- Primary goal: [Inform / Convert / Rank / Retain / Entertain]
- Specific outcome: [What measurable thing should happen]
- Success metric: [How we know it worked]

## Pillar / Strategy fit

- Content pillar this serves: [from content strategy]
- Cornerstone or supporting: [Cornerstone / Supporting]
- Internal links to / from: [related pieces]

## Angle

What's the take? Why does this piece exist when others on the topic already do?

- The angle: [One sentence]
- Why ours is different: [Specific differentiator]
- The contrarian or surprising element: [If any]

## Format

- Type: [Article / Guide / Listicle / Comparison / Case study / How-to / Tutorial / Explainer]
- Length: [Word count or range]
- Reading level: [Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced]
- Required elements: [Images, tables, video, downloads, code blocks]

## Structure

Section-by-section outline. Headlines + 1 sentence on what each section does.

### [H1: Working title]

[Hook approach: how the piece will earn the read in 3 to 5 seconds]

### H2: [Section title]
[What this section accomplishes]

### H2: [Section title]
[What this section accomplishes]

### H2: [Section title]
[What this section accomplishes]

### H2: [Closing section]
[How the piece resolves and what action it points to]

## Voice

- Apply: [Brand voice attributes]
- Tone for this piece: [How tone adjusts for the topic]
- Specific cues: [Anything to lean into or avoid for this topic]

## Sources and citations

- Required sources: [Specific data, studies, or experts to cite]
- Internal data: [Anything we have that competitors don't]
- Quotes from: [SMEs, customers, founders to include]

## SEO

- Primary keyword: [from keyword research]
- Secondary keywords: [supporting terms]
- Search intent: [Informational / Commercial / Transactional / Navigational]
- Target SERP feature (if any): [Featured snippet / People also ask / etc.]
- Title tag (50-60 chars): [Draft]
- Meta description (120-160 chars): [Draft]
- URL slug: [Draft]

## Distribution

Where and how this piece gets seen after publishing.

- Newsletter inclusion: [Yes/No]
- Social posts: [Number of variations and channels]
- Outreach: [Specific people or sites to ping]
- Internal links to add from: [Existing pieces that should link to this]

## Constraints and notes

- Deadline: [Date]
- Word count: [Target]
- Required approvals: [Who needs to sign off]
- Things to avoid: [Anything off-limits for this piece]

## Out of scope

What this piece is explicitly NOT about. Prevents scope creep during writing.

- [Topic not in this piece]
- [Topic not in this piece]
```

## Filled example

```markdown
# Brief: How to choose between SQL and NoSQL for a startup

## Reader

- Primary audience segment: Technical founders or first engineers at early-stage startups (under 20 employees)
- What they currently know: Basics of databases, used both SQL and NoSQL casually
- What they want to know: Which to pick for their specific situation, what they'll regret in 18 months
- What they need to do after: Make the database choice and feel confident enough to commit

## Goal

- Primary goal: Inform (with rank as a secondary)
- Specific outcome: Reader makes the decision and references this piece when others ask
- Success metric: Top 3 ranking on "SQL vs NoSQL for startups" within 6 months; 5+ minute average read time

## Pillar / Strategy fit

- Content pillar: Startup engineering decisions
- Cornerstone or supporting: Cornerstone for the pillar
- Internal links: from "Founder's guide to technical debt" and to "When to migrate your database"

## Angle

- The angle: Most articles compare SQL and NoSQL on technical features. We compare on regret-likelihood at 18 months in production.
- Why ours is different: Built around real failure modes, not theoretical capabilities
- Surprising element: Most startups regret choosing NoSQL for the wrong reasons, not the right ones

## Format

- Type: Long-form decision guide
- Length: 2,500 to 3,500 words
- Reading level: Intermediate
- Required elements: Decision flowchart, 2-3 case examples, comparison table

## Structure

### H1: How to choose between SQL and NoSQL for a startup

Hook: Open with a specific founder's regret moment from a real conversation.

### H2: The wrong question (and the right one)
Most teams ask "which is faster" or "which scales." Reframe to "what will I regret in 18 months."

### H2: When SQL is the right call
Specific scenarios with examples.

### H2: When NoSQL is the right call
Narrower scenarios than most articles claim.

### H2: The hybrid that works
Real-world pattern: SQL primary, targeted NoSQL for specific use cases.

### H2: A decision flowchart
The actionable diagram. The piece's main asset.

### H2: What to do if you've already chosen wrong
Migration realism, with specific signals it's time to switch.

### H2: Closing
Return to the opening founder. Where they ended up. The decision framework as the takeaway.

## Voice

- Apply: Direct, experienced, not-academic
- Tone: Slightly weary about industry overcomplication
- Specific cues: First-person plural ("we"), war-story examples preferred over textbook explanations

## Sources

- Internal: Conversations with [N] startup CTOs
- External: [List of specific studies, posts, papers]
- Quotes: [Names if any]

## SEO

- Primary keyword: SQL vs NoSQL startup
- Secondary: SQL vs NoSQL for small teams, when to use NoSQL, NoSQL mistakes startups
- Search intent: Informational with commercial undertone
- Target SERP feature: Featured snippet for "when to use NoSQL"
- Title tag: SQL vs NoSQL for Startups: How to Avoid 18-Month Regret
- Meta: A practical guide to picking a database when you're 5 engineers, deciding fast, and don't want to migrate twice. Includes a decision flowchart.
- URL slug: sql-vs-nosql-startup

## Distribution

- Newsletter: Feature in next issue
- Social: 3 LinkedIn variations, 1 X thread
- Outreach: Email to 5 startup engineering newsletter writers
- Internal links: Add from /resources/cto-decisions and /technical-debt-guide

## Constraints

- Deadline: [Date]
- Word count: 3,000 target
- Approvals: Editorial lead and one technical SME
- Avoid: Vendor-specific recommendations (we're not selling a product)

## Out of scope

- Specific NoSQL database comparisons (CouchDB vs MongoDB) - separate piece
- Schema design tutorials - separate piece
- ORM choice - separate piece
```

---

## When to skip the brief

The brief is required for:
- Cornerstone content
- Anything over 1500 words
- Anything with a conversion goal
- Anything written by someone other than the editorial lead

The brief can be lighter for:
- Short newsletter blurbs
- Social posts
- Pieces written by the editorial lead with the strategy fully internalized

But "lighter" is not "absent." Even a 100-word note covering reader / goal / angle saves time.
