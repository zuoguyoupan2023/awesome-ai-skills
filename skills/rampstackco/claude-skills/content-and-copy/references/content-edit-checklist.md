# Content Edit Checklist

A pre-publish checklist for editing content. Run it line by line before any piece of content goes live.

The checklist catches the regular mistakes. Editing for substance is a separate pass.

---

## Pass 1: substance

Before sweating commas, confirm the piece does its job.

- [ ] **The brief is satisfied.** Re-read the content brief. Does this piece deliver against every objective?
- [ ] **The audience is the right one.** Who is the reader? Are they assumed correctly?
- [ ] **The single takeaway is clear.** If a reader skims and remembers one thing, what is it? Is it what we wanted?
- [ ] **The first 50 words earn the next 50.** Cut throat-clearing intros. Get to the point.
- [ ] **Every paragraph earns its place.** If a paragraph could be removed without loss, remove it.
- [ ] **The structure serves the reader.** Headers form a meaningful outline. Skim test passes.
- [ ] **There is one CTA per piece, and it is unmissable.** Or zero, if the piece is purely informational.
- [ ] **No claim is unsupported.** Every fact, statistic, or assertion has a source or is hedged.
- [ ] **Examples are concrete.** Specific numbers, named scenarios, real artifacts beat vague "many users" generalizations.

---

## Pass 2: voice and tone

- [ ] **Voice matches the brand voice document.** Read aloud. If a stranger could not place this as our brand, voice is too generic.
- [ ] **Tone matches the context.** Promotional vs editorial vs technical vs support. Different content, different tone shifts.
- [ ] **Pronouns are consistent.** "You" / "we" / "our" stay stable across the piece.
- [ ] **No internal voice leaked into customer-facing copy.** Jargon, project names, internal team names removed.
- [ ] **No first-person commentary in instructional content.** ("I think you should..." → "Do this because...")

---

## Pass 3: structure and scannability

- [ ] **Headings are descriptive, not clever.** "How to add a payment method" beats "Money matters."
- [ ] **Heading hierarchy is correct.** One H1, then H2s for top-level sections, H3s under H2s. No skipping levels.
- [ ] **Paragraphs are tight.** Default 2-4 sentences per paragraph for web. One-sentence paragraphs are allowed for emphasis.
- [ ] **Lists are used where they fit.** Bullets for parallel items, numbered for sequenced steps.
- [ ] **Bold and italic used sparingly.** Bold for the term being defined, italic for emphasis. Not both. Not often.
- [ ] **Code blocks for code.** Inline code for short references.
- [ ] **Tables for tabular data.** Not for layout.

---

## Pass 4: clarity at the sentence level

- [ ] **Active voice by default.** Passive voice for specific reasons (focus on object, unknown actor, polite distancing).
- [ ] **Concrete nouns and verbs.** "We process" beats "processing is performed by us."
- [ ] **Average sentence length under 20 words.** Vary the rhythm with shorter sentences for emphasis.
- [ ] **No nominalizations.** "Decide" beats "make a decision." "Implement" beats "carry out the implementation of."
- [ ] **No throat-clearing phrases.** Cut "It is important to note that," "In order to," "At the end of the day."
- [ ] **No filler intensifiers.** Cut "very," "really," "quite," "actually," "basically." Each weakens the word it modifies.
- [ ] **No hedge words where you mean something specific.** "May," "might," "could" are honest when uncertain, weak when specific.

---

## Pass 5: jargon and accessibility

- [ ] **Jargon defined on first use.** Or replaced if the audience would not know it.
- [ ] **Acronyms spelled out on first use.** Then acronym is fine.
- [ ] **No insider language.** Project codenames, internal team references, deprecated product names.
- [ ] **Idioms checked for global audiences.** "Hit it out of the park" reads strange to non-US English readers.
- [ ] **Reading level appropriate.** Use a tool (Hemingway, Readable) to spot-check. For most audiences, target Grade 8-10.
- [ ] **No ableist or exclusionary language.** "Crazy" → "wild," "blacklist" → "blocklist," gendered defaults checked.

---

## Pass 6: SEO basics

If the piece is for organic search:

- [ ] **Primary query target identified.** One phrase per piece.
- [ ] **Title tag drafted.** Distinct from H1, 50-60 characters, primary query near front.
- [ ] **Meta description drafted.** 140-160 characters, value-led, includes primary query naturally.
- [ ] **H1 is unique on the site and includes the primary query.**
- [ ] **Internal links added.** 2-5 contextual links to related content on the site.
- [ ] **External links added.** Link out where it adds value or substantiates a claim.
- [ ] **Images have descriptive alt text.** Decorative images get `alt=""`.
- [ ] **URL slug is short, lowercase, hyphenated, includes primary query.**
- [ ] **Schema considered.** Article, FAQ, HowTo, etc. - whichever fits the format.

---

## Pass 7: facts and claims

- [ ] **Numbers verified.** Especially statistics, dates, prices, version numbers.
- [ ] **Names spelled correctly.** People, products, companies, places.
- [ ] **Quotes verified verbatim.** Speaker name, attribution, context correct.
- [ ] **Dates checked.** "Last updated" reflects the actual edit date.
- [ ] **Links work and go where they say.** No 404s, no redirect chains, no link rot to dead resources.
- [ ] **Screenshots are current.** Older screenshots show outdated UI; refresh or remove.
- [ ] **Claims about competitors are fair and current.** Or removed.

---

## Pass 8: legal and compliance

- [ ] **Trademarked terms used correctly.** Capitalization, ™ or ® where required by license.
- [ ] **Disclaimers present where needed.** Affiliate disclosures, regulated industry disclaimers, advisory caveats.
- [ ] **Customer permissions secured for testimonials.** Case studies, quotes, photos.
- [ ] **No confidential or pre-announcement information.** Product roadmaps, unannounced pricing, internal team names.
- [ ] **Image rights clear.** Licensed stock, original photography, or in-house illustration.

---

## Pass 9: cross-channel consistency

If the piece appears in more than one place:

- [ ] **Web vs email vs social variants align in message.** Tone may shift; substance does not contradict.
- [ ] **Excerpts and meta descriptions match the piece.** No bait-and-switch.
- [ ] **CTAs point to current destinations.** Landing page is live, has matching messaging, is not stale.

---

## Pass 10: final mechanical sweep

- [ ] **Spellcheck.** Twice. Once with autocorrect off so it does not silently fix names.
- [ ] **Grammar check.** Tool-assisted, then human-verified.
- [ ] **Punctuation consistent.** Oxford comma, smart quotes, dashes - per the style guide.
- [ ] **No double spaces.** Find-and-replace.
- [ ] **No trailing whitespace at line ends.**
- [ ] **No placeholder text left in.** "Lorem ipsum," "TBD," "[INSERT QUOTE]," "TKTK."
- [ ] **No commented-out drafts in the source.** Especially in CMS HTML view.
