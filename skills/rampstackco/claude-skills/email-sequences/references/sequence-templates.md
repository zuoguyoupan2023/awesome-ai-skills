# Sequence Templates

Starter outlines for the 5 standard sequence types. These are scaffolds. Adapt the cadence, voice, and content to the specific brand and audience.

---

## Onboarding sequence (SaaS, 7 emails over 14 days)

### Email 1 - Welcome
**Send:** Immediately on signup
**Subject:** Welcome to [Product]. Here's your first step.
**Body skeleton:**
- One-line welcome
- Restate the outcome they signed up for
- ONE clear next step
- Optional: founder note for early-stage products

### Email 2 - First action tutorial
**Send:** T+1 day
**Subject:** [Specific first task] in 5 minutes
**Body skeleton:**
- Hook: Why this first task matters
- 3 to 5 numbered steps
- Visual or screenshot
- CTA back to the product

### Email 3 - Build on first
**Send:** T+3 days
**Subject:** Now that [first task done], try [second task]
**Body skeleton:**
- Acknowledge progress (works even if user hasn't done first task; it primes them)
- Introduce next capability
- Show what it unlocks
- CTA

### Email 4 - Use case spotlight
**Send:** T+5 days
**Subject:** How [persona] uses [Product] to [outcome]
**Body skeleton:**
- Specific persona description
- Their workflow with the product
- Specific result
- "Try this approach" CTA

### Email 5 - Common questions
**Send:** T+7 days
**Subject:** Quick answers to questions new users ask
**Body skeleton:**
- 3 to 5 FAQs with brief answers
- Links to fuller help docs
- CTA: open help center or chat with support

### Email 6 - Re-engagement check
**Send:** T+10 days (only if user is dormant)
**Subject:** Need a hand getting started?
**Body skeleton:**
- Acknowledge they may be busy
- Offer a 10-minute call or specific resource
- One simple action they can take in <2 minutes

### Email 7 - Hand-off to ongoing comms
**Send:** T+14 days
**Subject:** You're set up. Here's what to expect from us.
**Body skeleton:**
- Confirm onboarding complete
- Set expectations for future emails (frequency, content type)
- Offer preferences center
- Light next-step CTA

## Lifecycle (triggered, individual emails)

### Anniversary (annual)
**Subject:** Hard to believe - it's been a year
**Body:** Celebrate the milestone, surface specific achievements ("You shipped X projects this year"), suggest next-level use case.

### Plan upgrade nudge
**Subject:** You're hitting your [Plan] limits
**Body:** Specific data showing usage approaching limits, value of next tier, painless upgrade path.

### Inactivity check (30 days dormant)
**Subject:** We haven't seen you for a bit
**Body:** Light touch, restate value, surface a recent improvement, easy way to come back.

### Power user celebration (Y key actions)
**Subject:** You've crossed [Y] [actions]. Nice.
**Body:** Acknowledge the milestone, share advanced tips, invite to community or beta.

### Feature announcement (when relevant feature ships)
**Subject:** New: [feature]
**Body:** What it does, why we built it, how to start using it. Brief.

## Sales nurture (B2B, 9 emails over 28 days)

### Email 1 - Deliver the asset
**Send:** Immediately on opt-in
**Subject:** Your [asset name]
**Body:** Direct download link, brief intro to what's inside.

### Email 2 - Educational angle
**Send:** T+2 days
**Subject:** [Specific question the audience asks]
**Body:** Useful content piece tied to their problem. No pitch.

### Email 3 - Educational angle (different facet)
**Send:** T+4 days
**Subject:** [Adjacent question]
**Body:** Different angle on the same problem space.

### Email 4 - Customer story
**Send:** T+7 days
**Subject:** How [Customer] [achieved specific outcome]
**Body:** Specific company, specific outcome, specific approach. Their story, with brand as supporting role.

### Email 5 - Objection handling
**Send:** T+10 days
**Subject:** "I'm not sure [common objection]"
**Body:** Address the most common reason this audience hesitates. Honest, non-defensive.

### Email 6 - Demo invitation
**Send:** T+14 days
**Subject:** Want to see [Product] in action?
**Body:** 20-minute personalized demo offer. Specific outcome the demo will show.

### Email 7 - Strong proof + soft pitch
**Send:** T+18 days
**Subject:** [Specific result] in [specific time]
**Body:** Strongest case study, direct invitation to start a trial or call.

### Email 8 - Urgency
**Send:** T+21 days
**Subject:** [Deadline or limited offer]
**Body:** Clear offer with deadline. Real urgency only - manufactured urgency damages trust.

### Email 9 - Goodbye
**Send:** T+28 days
**Subject:** Last note from us
**Body:** Acknowledge the silence. Offer one last resource or chance to opt back in. Then suppress or move to long-term nurture list.

## Transactional templates

### Order confirmation
**Subject:** Your order #[number] is confirmed
**Body:**
- Order summary (items, totals, shipping address)
- Estimated delivery
- Order tracking link
- Support contact

Keep marketing minimal. People are looking for specific info.

### Password reset
**Subject:** Reset your password
**Body:**
- Brief acknowledgment of the request
- Reset link (with expiration time stated)
- "Did not request this?" instruction
- Support contact

No marketing in transactional. Keep it clean.

### Email verification
**Subject:** Verify your email
**Body:**
- One-line ask
- Big verify button
- "If you did not sign up..." opt-out
- Expiration time

### Abandoned cart
**Subject:** You left these in your cart
**Body:**
- Items left in cart with images
- Quick-resume link
- Optional: incentive (free shipping, small discount)
- Support contact

Send 1 email at T+1 hour, optional second at T+24 hours, NO third email (annoyance penalty exceeds recovery).

## Win-back (5 emails over 20 days)

### Email 1 - We miss you
**Subject:** Hey, it's been a while
**Body:** Acknowledge the lapse, brief reminder of what's possible, soft re-engage CTA.

### Email 2 - What's new
**Subject:** A few things have changed since you were last here
**Body:** Updates, new features, recent improvements. Specific.

### Email 3 - Specific incentive
**Subject:** Come back: [specific offer]
**Body:** Concrete incentive (discount, bonus, free month). Deadline.

### Email 4 - Last chance
**Subject:** Last call: [offer ends X]
**Body:** Final reminder of the offer. Clear deadline.

### Email 5 - Sunset
**Subject:** We'll miss you
**Body:** Acknowledge they're not coming back, confirm we're stepping down sends, leave the door open.

After this email: suppress from active sends. Optional: move to a quarterly "big news only" list.

## Cadence rules across all sequence types

- **Default time of day:** 8 to 10 AM in the recipient's timezone for non-time-sensitive sends.
- **Avoid send fatigue:** Cap at 2 emails per week from the same brand to the same person across all programs.
- **Respect engagement:** If a user has opened zero of the last 5 emails, throttle frequency or sunset.
- **Honor unsubscribes immediately:** Same-day, all programs, no exceptions.
- **Test sending:** Send tests to 5+ devices before any sequence goes live.

## Subject line patterns that work

- **Specific question:** "Why your churn rate isn't moving"
- **Specific number:** "47% of B2B buyers do this before purchasing"
- **Personal/conversational:** "Quick question about [topic]"
- **Curiosity gap:** "The mistake we see in 80% of pricing pages"
- **Negative framing (sparingly):** "Don't make this mistake on your next launch"
- **Direct value:** "Free template: 90-day onboarding checklist"

Avoid:

- Subject lines that look like spam (all caps, multiple exclamations)
- Misleading subject lines (mentions in subject that aren't in body)
- Tricks ("Re:" or "Fwd:" on cold sends)
- Subject lines longer than 50 characters where avoidable

## Metrics to track per sequence

| Metric | Target benchmark (rough) |
|---|---|
| Open rate | 20 to 35% (varies wildly by industry) |
| Click-through rate | 2 to 5% |
| Conversion to goal | Sequence-specific |
| Unsubscribe rate per email | <0.5% |
| Spam complaint rate | <0.1% |
| Reply rate (for cold/B2B) | 1 to 3% |

Below benchmark: investigate the weakest emails in the sequence first. Above: capture what worked and apply to other sequences.
