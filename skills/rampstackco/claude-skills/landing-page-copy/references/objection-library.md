# Objection Library

Common objections by category, with handling strategies. Use this to pressure-test landing page copy and to make sure each major objection is met somewhere on the page.

If a landing page does not name and answer the audience's real objections, it will not convert no matter how strong the hero is.

---

## How to use this library

1. Identify your audience and the specific decision they are facing.
2. Walk this library and flag every objection that applies.
3. For each flagged objection, decide where on the page it gets handled (and whether it gets handled with copy, social proof, a guarantee, an FAQ, or a feature).
4. Pressure-test the page: read it as a skeptical reader. Are the flagged objections answered before the CTA?

Most pages handle 5-8 objections. Pages that handle 0-2 read as too good to be true. Pages that handle 15+ feel defensive.

## Category 1: trust

The audience does not know if you are real, competent, or honest.

### "I have never heard of you"

- **Symptom:** Bounce rate high, low time on page.
- **Handling:** Logo bar of customers, press mentions, founder photo and one-line bio, "since [year]" line, recognizable case study, security badges if relevant.

### "How do I know this works?"

- **Symptom:** Visitors read the whole page but do not act.
- **Handling:** Specific case studies with real names and specific outcomes ("X reduced Y by Z%"). Demo or trial. Money-back guarantee. Free assessment.

### "How do I know you will be around in a year?"

- **Symptom:** Common in B2B with switching costs, regulated industries, long-term commitments.
- **Handling:** Funding announcements, customer count, years in business, prominent enterprise customers, industry awards or compliance attestations.

### "Who is behind this?"

- **Symptom:** Common in personal brands, consultancies, or expertise-based services.
- **Handling:** Founder photo, credentials, prior work, authentic origin story.

## Category 2: fit

The audience cannot tell if this is for them.

### "Is this for someone like me?"

- **Symptom:** General-audience visitors converting; specific-segment visitors bouncing.
- **Handling:** Audience-specific copy, role-based or industry-based pages, named segments in the hero ("for [role] at [company size]"), audience-specific case studies.

### "Will this work for my situation?"

- **Symptom:** Visitors get to features and bounce.
- **Handling:** Use case sections, conditional FAQ ("if you are X, here is how this works"), filterable case studies, decision tree or quiz.

### "Is my problem big enough to need this?"

- **Symptom:** SMB visitors looking at enterprise products, or vice versa.
- **Handling:** Plan or tier comparison, "right-sized for [size]" messaging, alternatives section ("if you have [smaller problem], try [lighter approach]").

### "I have already solved this with [alternative]"

- **Symptom:** Visitors compare and leave.
- **Handling:** Comparison page, "vs [competitor]" page, "in addition to" framing, integrations section.

---

## Category 3: complexity and time

The audience worries this will be hard, slow, or distracting.

### "How long will this take to set up?"

- **Symptom:** Time-on-page high; conversion low. Common in dev tools, SaaS.
- **Handling:** "5 minutes to set up" messaging, video walkthrough showing real timing, testimonials from peers about onboarding, white-glove option.

### "Will I have to migrate or change what I am doing?"

- **Symptom:** Switching anxiety. Common in any tool replacement.
- **Handling:** Migration guides, importer features highlighted, "works alongside [current tool]" framing, migration assistance offered.

### "Will my team need training?"

- **Symptom:** Buyer is enthusiastic; champion is worried about adoption.
- **Handling:** Onboarding program, in-product training, role-based docs, video library, account manager for enterprise.

### "Is this going to add to my plate?"

- **Symptom:** Visitors are already overworked. Common pitch to operators, ICs.
- **Handling:** Time-saving framing with specific numbers ("saves 4 hours per week"), automation highlighted, replace-not-add positioning.

## Category 4: cost

The audience worries about price, value, or hidden costs.

### "How much does this cost?"

- **Symptom:** Anyone visiting the pricing page bounces. Conversion only on free trial.
- **Handling:** Transparent pricing on the marketing site (or "starts at" if pricing varies), no "request a quote" for low-tier pricing, ROI calculator.

### "Is it worth the price?"

- **Symptom:** Pricing page visited; nothing happens.
- **Handling:** Specific ROI examples ("customers see X return in Y months"), comparison to alternative ("instead of hiring a [role], you get [tool]"), free trial or money-back, value framing throughout.

### "Are there hidden fees?"

- **Symptom:** Visitors abandon at checkout or in sales calls when extras come up.
- **Handling:** "No setup fees" line, "no per-seat charges" if true, "all features included" if true, transparent pricing page with all variables surfaced.

### "I do not have budget right now"

- **Symptom:** Common in macro slowdowns. Visitors say "interesting, send info."
- **Handling:** Annual discount, smaller plan, free tier, ROI-led copy, ROI calculator, "self-funding within X months" claim if defensible.

---

## Category 5: risk

The audience fears what happens if this goes wrong.

### "What if it does not work?"

- **Symptom:** Visitors explore deeply but do not commit.
- **Handling:** Money-back guarantee, free trial, free pilot, money-back specifically tied to outcome ("if you do not see X, we refund"), case studies of customers who got results.

### "What if my data is at risk?"

- **Symptom:** Common in B2B, data tools, financial, healthcare.
- **Handling:** Security page linked from footer (and from sales-relevant pages), compliance badges (SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, HIPAA), data-handling explainer, "your data is yours" framing.

### "What if I am locked in?"

- **Symptom:** Visitors ask about export, contracts, cancellation in sales calls.
- **Handling:** "Export your data anytime," "no long-term contract," "cancel any time," month-to-month option visible.

### "What if my team will not adopt it?"

- **Symptom:** Champion deals stall.
- **Handling:** Onboarding program, role-based pricing or trials, internal-rollout playbook, customer testimonials from peer companies on adoption.

---

## Category 6: timing

The audience does not see urgency.

### "I will get to this later"

- **Symptom:** Sign-ups but no follow-through. Long sales cycles.
- **Handling:** Cost-of-inaction framing ("each month you wait costs X"), time-bound offer (tasteful, real, not fake), seasonal hook if relevant, before-and-after framing showing time loss.

### "I am evaluating multiple options"

- **Symptom:** Pipeline takes 6+ months to close.
- **Handling:** Comparison page, evaluation guide ("how to evaluate [category]"), trial that gets the buyer hands-on quickly, customer references for direct calls.

---

## Category 7: politics and process

The audience cannot decide alone.

### "I need to get my boss to approve this"

- **Symptom:** Champion engagement high; deal stuck above champion's authority.
- **Handling:** Business case template, ROI calculator, exec one-pager, "make the case to [role]" content, customer references at peer companies (named role buying the same tool).

### "I need legal/security/procurement to review"

- **Symptom:** Long lag between handshake and contract signature.
- **Handling:** Security questionnaire pre-completed, MSA boilerplate available, vendor onboarding kit, named legal/security contact for buyer-side reviewers.

### "I need to align with [other team]"

- **Symptom:** Multi-stakeholder deals stall.
- **Handling:** Team-specific pages, team-specific demos, multi-stakeholder ROI framing, internal advocacy kit (slide deck, FAQ).

---

## Category 8: emotional / status

The audience worries about how they will look.

### "What if this is the wrong call?"

- **Symptom:** Decision-maker stalls. Common with high-visibility purchases.
- **Handling:** Peer customer testimonials ("[Title] at [Recognizable Company] said X"), industry analyst recognition, awards, money-back guarantee that limits downside.

### "Will this make me look behind / ahead?"

- **Symptom:** First-mover anxiety or laggard anxiety. Industry-specific.
- **Handling:** "Industry standard now" framing for laggards, "ahead of the curve" framing for first-movers, social proof from peer companies in either case.

## How objection handling shows up on the page

The library is the inventory. The page is where it lands. The most common placements:

- **Hero:** answers the "is this for me" objection.
- **Logo bar:** answers "are you real."
- **Feature sections:** answer "does this do what I need."
- **Use case sections:** answer "will this work for my situation."
- **Case study sections:** answer "does this work."
- **Comparison sections:** answer "what about alternatives."
- **Pricing section:** answers "what does it cost."
- **FAQ section:** answers the long tail of objections.
- **Guarantee / trial offer:** answers "what if it does not work."
- **Footer trust block:** answers "is my data safe" via compliance badges.

If an objection from your audience is not answered anywhere on the page, the page is incomplete.

## Worked example

**Audience:** Engineering manager at a series-B startup, 30-50 person engineering org, evaluating an observability tool to replace an in-house Grafana setup.

**Top 5 objections (filtered from the library):**

1. *"How long will this take to set up?"* (Category: complexity)
2. *"Will I have to migrate?"* (Category: complexity)
3. *"What does this cost at our scale?"* (Category: cost)
4. *"What if my engineers do not adopt it?"* (Category: risk + politics)
5. *"What if our data is at risk?"* (Category: risk)

**Page placements:**

| Objection | Where it gets handled |
|---|---|
| Setup time | Hero ("Live in 10 minutes" + 60-second product video) |
| Migration | Migration section ("imports from Grafana automatically") + case study showing migration story |
| Cost at scale | Pricing page with engineer-count slider, transparent monthly cost |
| Adoption | Adoption section showing in-product onboarding, plus a customer quote ("80% of engineers logged in week one") |
| Data risk | Footer trust block (SOC 2, ISO, GDPR) + a "your data" explainer linked from pricing |

Page is built around answering these five, in order, from hero to CTA.
