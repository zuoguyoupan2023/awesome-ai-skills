# Example variant spec

A fully populated single-variant spec for a fictitious brand, **Atlas Coffee**. Atlas is a hypothetical specialty coffee company sourcing single-origin beans from Latin American producers, roasting in small batches, retailing through a single flagship cafe and an online subscription. The brand brief calls for considered craft positioning, outdoor adjacency without literal outdoor imagery, warmth without rusticity.

This file shows what one variant looks like when documented for client review. A complete deliverable contains 6 to 12 such variants. The agent uses this format as the per-variant template; the client uses it to evaluate, compare, and select.

---

## Variant 07, Mountain peak A lockup, IBM Plex Sans

### Architecture

Lockup. The wordmark sits to the right of a custom letterform-as-symbol "A". The "A" reads as both the first letter of "Atlas" and as a stylized mountain peak. The lockup also has a stacked alternate (the "A" centered above the wordmark) for square contexts and a symbol-only fallback (the "A" alone) for favicon, embroidery, and stamping.

### Typography

- **Wordmark typeface.** IBM Plex Sans, Medium weight (500), tracking +5 (10 units, on a 1000-unit em).
- **Custom letterform.** The "A" is a custom drawing, not IBM Plex Sans. The crossbar is replaced with a single horizontal stroke at 38 percent height; the apex rises to a sharp peak; the legs are slightly splayed (8 degrees from vertical) to suggest mountain base width without exaggeration. The custom "A" replaces the typed "A" in the wordmark "Atlas Coffee" so the entire wordmark reads as a unit.
- **Wordmark composition.** "Atlas Coffee" set as one word, with a small visual gap between "Atlas" and "Coffee" (one half x-height of additional spacing) so the eye reads both words as parallel parts of the same name without forcing a hyphen or stacked composition.
- **Optical adjustments.** The "t" of "Atlas" is shortened by 4 units to balance against the rising "A" to its left. The "f" of "Coffee" has its terminal pulled inward by 3 units to prevent it from reading as an extra bar.

### Symbol description

The custom "A" is the symbol. The construction is built on a 4-unit grid:

- Apex height: 4 units, sharp peak
- Crossbar: at 1.5 unit height, single horizontal stroke
- Leg width at base: 4 units, splayed 8 degrees from vertical
- Stroke weight: matches the wordmark's stroke weight at the chosen Medium weight (approximately 90 units on a 1000-unit em)

The peak silhouette is ungenerous on the apex (sharp, not rounded). The crossbar is single-stroke (no double bars). The legs end with a flat foot, not a serif. At every scale, the silhouette reads first as a peak, then as the letter A. The double-reading test passes.

### Color tokens

- **Primary.** Warm charcoal `#2A2521`. The wordmark and the "A" symbol both use this color in standard reproduction.
- **Accent.** Rust `#B85C38`. The accent appears in marketing surfaces (web header backgrounds, packaging trim) but not in the lockup itself.
- **Single-color black.** `#000000`. For court filings is irrelevant; for embossed letterhead, foil stamp on paper, single-color print, fax (yes, still). The mark renders as pure black on white.
- **Single-color white.** `#FFFFFF`. For reverse-on-dark applications (web header in dark mode, signage at night, embossed on dark leather).
- **Reverse-on-dark variant.** White on a dark background; the symbol's negative space inversion is direction-neutral so the mark reads identically in either direction.

### Application notes

The variant excels at:
- Large-format signage. The peak silhouette reads at street scale; the wordmark holds at 8 to 12 feet wide.
- Retail packaging. The lockup at coffee bag scale (4 inches wide) is the natural size; the symbol carries the front, the wordmark anchors the side.
- Mug and ceramic stamping. The lockup with stacked alternate works on cylindrical surfaces.
- Embossed letterhead. The custom "A" embossed at the top, the wordmark printed below in standard color.
- Web header at desktop scale (200 to 400 pixels wide). The lockup reads cleanly at this size.

The variant needs fallbacks at:
- 16 px favicon. The full lockup fails; the symbol-only "A" carries.
- 1.5 inch embroidery patch. The lockup is too wide; the symbol-only "A" with the wordmark stacked below in a chunky weight (e.g., IBM Plex Sans Bold) is the patch variant.
- App icon at 28 to 60 pixels. The symbol-only "A" inside a colored field (warm charcoal background, white "A") is the app icon variant.
- Square social profile. The stacked alternate (the "A" above the wordmark, centered) is the social profile variant.

Embroidery adaptation: thread color matched to the warm charcoal primary (Madeira Polyneon `1842` is the closest match for the primary, with `1338` for the rust accent). The embroidery-grade variant of the symbol-only "A" requires a 3 unit minimum stroke weight scaled for 1.5 inch patch reproduction.

### Signals

- **Considered craft.** The wordmark's optical adjustments (shortened "t", pulled-in "f") signal that the brand has thought about every letterform, which signals that the brand thinks about every other detail.
- **Outdoor / adventure adjacency without literal outdoor imagery.** The mountain peak silhouette references outdoor without depicting a trail, a tree, or a campsite. The audience associates the brand with adventure-adjacent positioning without the brand becoming a Pacific-Northwest cliche.
- **Warmth without rusticity.** The warm charcoal primary and rust accent read as warm but considered. The palette avoids the heritage-coffee browns and the third-wave-minimalist whites; the in-between space signals contemporary craft.
- **Single-origin discipline.** The restrained execution (one symbol, one wordmark, one accent color) signals that the brand chooses few things deliberately. The same discipline appears in single-origin coffee sourcing.

### Rejects

- **Not a typical coffee shop laurel mark.** Atlas does not use a laurel wreath, a coffee bean, a coffee cup, or any direct depiction of coffee. The brand is about the experience and origin, not the commodity.
- **Not a third-wave-minimalist wordmark with no symbol.** Many specialty coffee brands have moved toward wordmark-only with no symbol. Atlas chooses a lockup because the brand wants a symbol-grade asset for embroidery, hardware stamping, and merchandise.
- **Not a literal coffee bean.** No bean, no berry, no plant in the symbol. The category cliche is rejected.
- **Not a Pacific-Northwest-cabin aesthetic.** No tree silhouette, no compass rose, no flannel-pattern background. The brand's outdoor adjacency is structural (peak silhouette in the letter), not stylistic.

### Mockups

The mockups would be rendered as image assets in a complete deliverable. Described in prose:

1. **Storefront signage at street scale.** The lockup as channel letters above the cafe entrance, illuminated, mounted on stained wood paneling. The peak silhouette reads from across the street; the wordmark anchors the brand name as visitors approach.

2. **Retail bag at shelf scale.** The 12 oz coffee bag with the lockup printed at 4 inches wide on the front, the wordmark-only with origin information on the side panel. The symbol's peak references the high-altitude growing regions where Atlas sources beans.

3. **Espresso cup at table scale.** Double-walled glass cup with the symbol-only "A" stamped in warm charcoal at 1 inch tall on the cup wall. The lockup is on the saucer rim. The customer sees both marks during a 4-minute espresso experience.

4. **Embroidered apron.** Black cotton apron with the symbol-only "A" embroidered in rust on the chest pocket. The wordmark is embroidered on the back at the lower hem, in warm charcoal thread, smaller scale. The aprons are durable goods that get washed weekly; the embroidery is built for that.

5. **Social profile picture as stacked mark.** The stacked alternate (the "A" above the wordmark, centered, white on warm charcoal background) renders cleanly inside Twitter/Instagram circle masks at 80 to 400 pixel display sizes.

## Notes for the agent reading this file

This single-variant spec is one of typically 6 to 12 in a complete deliverable. The brand owner reads through all variants, marks preferences, asks questions, and selects 1 to 3 finalists for production refinement.

The discipline in writing the spec:

- **Be specific.** "IBM Plex Sans Medium with tracking +5" is specific. "Modern sans-serif" is not.
- **Show the system.** Each variant is a primary mark plus its fallbacks. Document the fallback hierarchy, not just the primary.
- **Justify with signals and rejects.** What does the variant communicate? What does it explicitly NOT communicate? The negative space in the rejects sharpens the positive selection.
- **Render mockups, in image or in prose.** The brand owner needs to see the mark in context to evaluate. A spec without mockups is half-finished.
- **Prepare for production.** The color tokens, embroidery thread color matches, foil stamp specifications, and minimum sizes are production information. The spec is the bridge from decision to manufacture.

The output of this skill is decision material. The next step after delivery is selection. The step after that is production refinement on the chosen direction: cleaner construction, refined kerning, color system finalization, application library.
