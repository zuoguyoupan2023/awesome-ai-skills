# Outreach Templates

Templates for the most common off-page outreach scenarios. Personalize every send. The templates are scaffolding, not ready-to-fire copy.

A few rules across all templates:

- Subject lines under 60 characters
- First line never says "I love your blog" or any variant
- One specific reference to their actual work (not a generic compliment)
- One clear ask
- One reason it benefits them, not you
- Sign with a real name and link to a real profile
- Plain text, no formatting, no images, no tracking pixels

---

## Guest post pitch

**Subject:** [Topic] for [their site name]?

Hi [first name],

I just read your piece on [specific article, published Date] and noticed [specific observation about an angle they did not cover, or a question their piece raised but did not answer].

I write about [your topic area] and I think the gap there could become a strong follow-up. Working title: "[Specific working title]." It would cover:

- [Specific point 1]
- [Specific point 2]
- [Specific point 3]

I can have a draft to you in two weeks. Recent samples of my writing:
- [URL 1]
- [URL 2]

If this is not a fit, no worries. Let me know either way.

Thanks,
[Name]
[Title and where you write]

## Broken link outreach

**Subject:** Broken link on your [page topic] page

Hi [first name],

I was reading your [page name] page (great resource) and noticed the link to [specific old URL] is broken - the destination returns a 404.

I run [your site] and we have a piece on the same topic that might be a relevant replacement: [your URL]. It covers [specific overlap with what the broken link was for].

No pressure either way. Just wanted to flag the broken link.

[Name]

## Citation / mention reclamation

When your brand is mentioned online but not linked to.

**Subject:** Quick note on the [piece] you published

Hi [first name],

Thanks for mentioning [your brand] in your [piece title, with link]. Genuinely appreciated.

Quick request: would you be open to linking the mention back to [your URL]? Helps your readers find us, and helps us track the reach of the piece.

No worries if it is not your editorial standard to add links retroactively.

Thanks,
[Name]

## Universal don'ts

- Do not start with "Hope you are doing well."
- Do not call yourself a "fellow [profession]" unless you actually know them.
- Do not say "I'll keep this brief" and then write 600 words.
- Do not attach files in cold outreach.
- Do not follow up more than twice. Three+ follow-ups damage reputation.
- Do not use subject lines like "Quick question" or "Re: [topic]" if it is not actually a reply.
- Do not include phrases like "value-add," "synergy," or "leverage."
- Do not pitch yourself as the expert in their field. They are.

---

## Personalization checklist

Before sending any outreach:

- [ ] Used their first name correctly (spelled right)
- [ ] Referenced specific recent work of theirs (not from 5 years ago)
- [ ] Stated the ask clearly in the first 3 lines
- [ ] Gave them a reason to say yes that benefits them
- [ ] Plain text, no HTML, no images, no tracking pixel
- [ ] Subject line under 60 characters and not deceptive
- [ ] Signature includes real credentials and a way to verify the sender

If any are missing, do not send.
