# Mechanism Thinking (Why This Works)

## The Core Idea

People don’t buy results.

They buy explanations.

A mechanism explains:

* why previous attempts failed
* why this approach works
* why it works now

---

## The Structure

Every strong mechanism has 3 parts:

### 1. The Failure

Why what they tried didn’t work.

> “Running ads didn’t fail because ads don’t work…
> it failed because your messaging never matched buyer awareness.”

---

### 2. The Shift

What changed.

> “Once you align message to awareness stage…”

---

### 3. The Result

What becomes possible.

> “…your ads stop feeling like ads and start converting.”

---

## Types of Mechanisms

### 1. Process Mechanism

A new way of doing something

### 2. Discovery Mechanism

A hidden insight

### 3. System Mechanism

A structured framework

### 4. Timing Mechanism

Why it works now (not before)

---

## Naming Rules

* Short
* Memorable
* Ownable

Examples:

* “Awareness Matching System”
* “Offer Compression Method”
* “3-Second Hook Framework”

---

## The Real Role of a Mechanism

It does 3 things:

1. Creates belief
2. Differentiates the offer
3. Justifies the result

---

## Common Mistakes

* Not naming the mechanism
* Making it too complex
* Explaining features instead of causality

---

## Test

If someone hears your mechanism and thinks:

“I haven’t heard it explained like that before…”

You’re close.

If they think:

“That actually makes sense why mine didn’t work…”

You’ve got it.
