# Eugene Schwartz Awareness Framework (Applied)

## The Core Idea

People don’t buy based on your offer.

They buy based on what they already know.

If your message doesn’t match their awareness level, it fails — regardless of how good it is.

---

## The 5 Awareness Stages

### 1. Unaware

They don’t know they have a problem.

**Message Strategy:**

* Tell a story
* Surface a hidden issue
* Create curiosity

**What Works:**

* Pattern interrupts
* Emotional hooks
* Relatable scenarios

**What Fails:**

* Selling
* Explaining solutions too early

---

### 2. Problem Aware

They know something is wrong, but not how to fix it.

**Message Strategy:**

* Agitate the problem
* Deepen the pain
* Show consequences

**What Works:**

* “You’re not alone” positioning
* Specific pain articulation
* Future pacing (if nothing changes)

---

### 3. Solution Aware

They know solutions exist, but not yours.

**Message Strategy:**

* Introduce mechanisms
* Compare approaches
* Differentiate methods

**What Works:**

* “Most solutions fail because…”
* Breaking false beliefs
* New way vs old way framing

---

### 4. Product Aware

They know you, but are unsure.

**Message Strategy:**

* Proof
* Objection handling
* Risk reduction

**What Works:**

* Case studies
* Testimonials
* Demonstrations

---

### 5. Most Aware

They’re ready.

**Message Strategy:**

* Offer
* Urgency
* Call to action

**What Works:**

* Clear pricing
* Bonuses
* Scarcity

---

## Key Rules

1. You cannot skip stages
2. The more aware the audience, the shorter the copy
3. The less aware, the more you must lead them

---

## Common Mistakes

* Selling to unaware audiences
* Educating aware audiences
* Using the same message for all traffic

---

## Application to Paid Ads

Cold traffic → Problem aware or unaware
Warm traffic → Solution aware
Retargeting → Product aware
Hot lists → Most aware

---

## The Real Insight

Most campaigns fail not because the offer is bad…

…but because the message is talking to the wrong version of the buyer.
